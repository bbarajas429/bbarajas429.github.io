About Me

Professional Background

I graduated from the University of California, Santa Barbara in 2022 with a B.S. in Zoology. During that time, I assisted in a meta-analysis, extracting global data on ungulate diets to better understand how plant biodiversity is affected by select functional traits of large herbivores. After graduating, I held various positions within the public sector, working for the National Parks Service and later, the California Department of Food and Agriculture. It is through these experiences that I found a passion for the intersection between land management and integrated pest management.

I am currently pursuing a Master of Environmental Data Science at the UC Santa Barbara, Bren School of Environmental Science & Management. Moving forward, I hope to use remote sensing data to examine how forest ecosystem stressors, such as insect-transmitted pathogens, impact ecosystem function. My goal is to contribute to informed decision-making regarding the management of public lands.

Bird Watching

Part of my undergraduate coursework included a vertebrate lab that consisted primarily of bird watching. I quickly got hooked and purchased binoculars soon after completing the course. I keep track of most of my finds using the Merlin and eBird apps, which are maintained by the Cornell Lab of Ornithology.

I thought it would be fun to visualize my personal bird sightings using a leaflet map:

Code 
# add CRS to lat/long points
birds <- birds %>%   st_as_sf(coords = c('longitude', 'latitude'), crs = 'EPSG:4236')

# create map
birds %>% 
  leaflet() %>% 
  addProviderTiles(providers$Jawg.Streets,
                   options = providerTileOptions(accessToken = token)) %>% 
  addMarkers(label = birds$common_name,
             popup = paste0(birds$common_name, ' (',
                            birds$scientific_name, ')'),
             clusterOptions = markerClusterOptions(),
             icon = makeIcon(iconUrl = iconpath,
                             iconWidth = 32, iconHeight = 32))
